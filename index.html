About

I am a Ph.D. student in Economics at North Carolina State University, where I began my doctoral studies in August 2025. Before coming to NC State, I earned an MPhil in Agricultural Economics and a BSc. in Agribusiness Management, both from the Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology (KNUST) in Kumasi, Ghana.

My research lies at the intersection of agricultural, development and environmental economics, with a focus on improving the livelihoods of farmers. I use applied econometrics, impact evaluation methods, and increasingly machine learning to examine issues such as climate-smart agriculture, agricultural technology adoption, food security, agricultural finance, risk management, productivity, and rural development. My work seeks to generate rigorous evidence that informs policies promoting sustainable agricultural systems, poverty reduction, resilience to climate change, and inclusive economic development. Much of it draws on household- and farm-level survey data from Ghana and other Sub-Saharan African countries. My research has been published in leading international journals, including Review of Development Economics, Journal of Agricultural and Applied Economics, Applied Energy, Energy Policy, and Agricultural Finance Review.

I also serve as a reviewer for a range of journals including Food Policy, Applied Economics, Scientific Reports, and Heliyon.

Prior to my Ph.D., I worked as a research assistant and field enumerator on projects with organizations including the Alliance for Green Revolution for Africa (AGRA), the World Food Programme (WFP), UNIDO, and the Council for Scientific and Industrial Research (CSIR), and I completed an internship with the Environmental Defense Fund working on agricultural finance and climate-smart agriculture.
